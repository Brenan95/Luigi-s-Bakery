Welcome to Luigi's Bakery Website

A wonderful local bakery with housemade pastries and cakes



A. Documentation of the Development Process

The intent and purpose behind the website was to design a bakery site that had helpful

information and was interactive. This was part of my final assignment in my IT270 class

at Southern New Hampshire University. The prompt being to design a website for my uncle

that looks professional and shows proficiency with HTML and CSS. I spent the last few weeks

editing and organizing the code in a way to make it flow and work the way I needed it to.


B. Defense of the Final Product

I wanted the website to have a casual feel while also having features that display the bakeries

best attributes, which is the food. I chose to go with a background that is a creamy yellow and

went well with the logo colors, as well as the footer and headers. I didnâ€™t want the font to be too

busy on the page so I went with arial to give a nice clean look. I chose to make the font a maroon color

to go with the color scheme of the website and stand out nicely against the creamy yellow. I read that the

colors yellow and red had psychological effects on viewers and were supposed to trigger hunger responses in

the brain. I figured if I set my website up with these colors it might just get the bakery more business.

I was trying to think of the general audience of and who would be using the website so I wanted to make it

friendly and intuitive for all ages. There might be elderly who want to use the website to order so I wanted

it to be minimally usable with few clickable options to keep it simple.


C. Opportunities for Improvement and Growth

I kind of want to develop the overall look of the website more and add other features that could

make the ordering process more efficient. I feel I could have done the layout differently with

more time and made it even more interactive.Content wise I might want to add a menu to the

website to give customers a chance to look things over and decide what they want.
